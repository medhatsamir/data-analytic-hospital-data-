Introduction¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row. 1-‘ScheduledDay’ tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment. 2-‘Neighborhood’ indicates the location of the hospital. 3-‘Scholarship’ indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Família. 4-In column ‘No_show’ it says ‘No’ if the patient showed up to their appointment, and ‘Yes’ if they did not show up.

Conclusions¶

• The most crucial factor that determines whether a patient will show or not is their age; the average age of those who are most likely to show is 39, while the average age of those who are not likely to show is 35.

• Gender, alcoholism, or sending a text message for an appointment has no bearing on getting the patient to show up.

• 22.8 percent of people who scheduled an appointment but didn't show up, implying that the 1/4 of people are most likely to show up late.

Limitations¶

More data is missing that might be more beneficial in predicting the patient's show, such as weather, employment, or regional data.

There were some nonsensical facts, such as patients who were under the age of one.
